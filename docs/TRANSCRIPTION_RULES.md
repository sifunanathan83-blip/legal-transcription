# TranscribeMe Legal Formatting Rules

## Overview

This document outlines the legal transcription formatting standards used by the Legal Transcription App, following TranscribeMe conventions for court-ready transcripts.

## Speaker Labels

### Format

All speaker labels must be in ALL CAPS, followed by a colon:

```
THE COURT:
MR. ATTORNEY:
MS. ATTORNEY:
MR. WITNESS:
THE REPORTER:
THE BAILIFF:
```

### Examples

```
THE COURT: The court is now in session.

MR. ATTORNEY: Your Honor, I move to suppress.

MS. ATTORNEY: Objection, Your Honor.

MR. WITNESS: I was there on that date.
```

## Speaker Identification

Speakers must be clearly identified on their first appearance:

```
Q: Please state your name for the record.
A: My name is Robert Johnson.
Q: And what is your occupation?
A: I'm a physician.
```

## Q&A Format (Colloquies)

Questions and answers use "Q:" and "A:" format:

```
Q: Tell us what you observed.
A: I saw a blue car turn the corner.
Q: What time was this?
A: Approximately 3 p.m.
```

## Timestamps

### Time Notation Format

Timestamps appear in square brackets and include time notation:

```
[HH:MM:SS]
(Time notation: H:MM:SS a.m./p.m.)
```

### Placement

Timestamps are placed:
- At major transitions
- After approximately 60 seconds of dialogue
- At the beginning of a new speaker's statement

### Example

```
MR. ATTORNEY: Your Honor, I move to suppress the evidence.
[10:45:32]
(Time notation: 10:45:32 a.m.)
THE COURT: Overruled.
```

## Objections and Rulings

### Objection Format

```
MR. ATTORNEY: Objection, [type of objection].
```

### Common Objections

- Objection, assumes facts not in evidence.
- Objection, compound question.
- Objection, vague and ambiguous.
- Objection, calls for speculation.
- Objection, hearsay.
- Objection, improper foundation.
- Objection, relevance.
- Objection, best evidence rule.
- Objection, form of the question.
- Objection, asked and answered.

### Rulings

```
THE COURT: Sustained.
THE COURT: Overruled.
THE COURT: Withdrawn.
THE COURT: Noted.
```

### Example

```
Q: What did the defendant say to you?
MR. ATTORNEY: Objection, hearsay.
THE COURT: Sustained.
The witness will not answer.
```

## Procedural Markers

Procedural events are noted in parentheses:

```
(Whereupon, a brief recess was taken.)
(Whereupon, the proceeding was adjourned.)
(Whereupon, the witness was sworn.)
(Whereupon, the jury was excused.)
(Whereupon, the examination continued.)
```

### Example

```
THE COURT: We'll adjourn for lunch. We'll reconvene at 2 p.m.
(Whereupon, a lunch recess was taken.)
(Whereupon, the proceeding reconvened at 2 p.m.)
THE COURT: The court is now back in session.
```

## Court Reporter Notations

Special notations appear in parentheses:

```
(Pause)
(Brief pause)
(Long pause)
(Inaudible)
(Unintelligible)
(Off the record)
(On the record)
(Simultaneous speaking)
(Crosstalk)
(Speaking)
```

### Example

```
Q: What happened next?
(Pause)
A: Well, the car stopped at the light.
```

## Exhibits

### Marking Exhibits

```
Q: I'm showing you what's been marked as Exhibit 1. Do you recognize it?
A: Yes, I do.
Q: What is Exhibit 1?
A: That's the purchase agreement.
```

### Reference Format

```
Exhibit 1: Purchase Agreement
Exhibit 2: Witness Statement
Exhibit A: Photograph
```

### Example

```
Q: I'm showing you Exhibit 1, which is the purchase agreement dated January 15, 2024. Is this the agreement you signed?
A: Yes, this is the agreement.
Q: And you signed on this line?
A: That's correct.
```

## Phonetic Spellings

### Unknown or Unusual Terms

```
[phonetic: BLURGENSTEIN]
[phonetic: MONEE-PULL or MON-O-POLE]
```

### Example

```
Q: What is the name of the company?
A: It's Johnson-Feldstein, [phonetic: FELD-STINE].
Q: How do you spell that?
A: F-E-L-D-S-T-E-I-N.
```

## Names and Proper Nouns

### First Mention

Names and proper nouns are fully capitalized on first mention:

```
The witness, ROBERT JOHNSON, testified that he was present.
```

### Subsequent References

After first mention, use standard capitalization:

```
Q: Mr. Johnson, were you present at that time?
A: Yes, I was.
```

## Numbers and Dates

### Numbers

- Spell out numbers zero through nine
- Use numerals for 10 and above
- Exception: spell out when starting a sentence

```
The witness testified on three separate occasions.
There were 15 people in the room.
Fifty-two witnesses testified.
```

### Dates

```
January 15, 2024
the 15th of January
```

## Page Breaks and Formatting

### Page Length

- Standard: 25-30 lines per page
- Maintains readability
- Allows for page numbers and headers

### Line Numbers

Optional: Add line numbers on left margin for easy reference:

```
1    THE COURT: We are now in session.
2    
3    MR. ATTORNEY: Your Honor, I move to dismiss.
4    
5    THE COURT: The motion is denied.
```

## Formatting Requirements

### Margins
- Left: 1 inch
- Right: 0.5 inch
- Top: 1 inch
- Bottom: 1 inch

### Font
- Courier New or similar monospace font
- 12-point size
- Single-spaced

### Indentation
- Paragraphs: 5 spaces
- Speaker labels: No indentation
- Continuation of speaker statement: 5 spaces

### Line Spacing
- Single-spaced within testimony
- Double-spaced between speakers

## Example Complete Segment

```
                          DISTRICT COURT OF CALIFORNIA
                              COUNTY OF LOS ANGELES

Smith v. Johnson

Case Number: CASE-2024-001234

--- Deposition Transcript ---

Date: June 25, 2024
Judge: Hon. John Smith
Court Reporter: Jane Doe

                           EXAMINATION BY MR. ATTORNEY

Q: Please state your name for the record.

A: My name is Robert Johnson.

Q: Mr. Johnson, were you present on January 15, 2024?

MR. ATTORNEY: Objection, assumes facts not in evidence.

THE COURT: Sustained. Rephrase the question.

Q: Were you present on January 15, 2024?

A: Yes, I was.

Q: Please describe what you observed.

A: I saw a blue sedan turn the corner at the intersection of
Main and Fifth Street.

(Pause)

Q: What time was this?

A: It was approximately 3 p.m.

[15:30:00]
(Time notation: 3:30:00 p.m.)

(Whereupon, a brief recess was taken.)
```

## Style Guidelines

1. **Consistency**: Use consistent formatting throughout
2. **Clarity**: Ensure all speakers are clearly identified
3. **Accuracy**: Verify proper spellings and pronunciations
4. **Completeness**: Include all colloquies and procedural markers
5. **Professional**: Maintain court-ready appearance

## Common Errors to Avoid

- ❌ Using lowercase for speaker labels
- ❌ Using quotation marks instead of Q:/A: format
- ❌ Inconsistent timestamp formatting
- ❌ Missing objection rulings
- ❌ Spelling proper names incorrectly
- ❌ Omitting procedural markers
- ❌ Unclear speaker identification

- [ ] Phonetic spellings marked
